# Journal Style Revision Checklist

## Goal
Revise the manuscript so that it reads like a publishable journal review article rather than a collection of notes or section summaries.

---

## Global Priorities
- strengthen review-style synthesis
- improve academic tone
- reduce repetition
- unify terminology
- clarify the distinction between theory and practice
- improve section transitions
- insert citation placeholders where needed

---

## Style Rules
### Keep
- formal academic tone
- concise paragraph-based writing
- balanced discussion of progress and limitations
- explicit comparison where relevant

### Avoid
- hype language
- vague praise
- unsupported strong claims
- paper-by-paper listing
- casual or note-like phrasing

### Prefer
- “under specific assumptions”
- “has shown theoretical advantages”
- “remains challenging in practice”
- “resource requirements remain substantial”
- “the practical significance remains unsettled”

---

## Section Checks

### Abstract
- [ ] clearly states scope
- [ ] summarizes both progress and constraints
- [ ] sounds like a review abstract
- [ ] avoids broad unsupported claims

### Introduction
- [ ] explains why the review is needed
- [ ] defines the scope clearly
- [ ] introduces the theory-practice tension
- [ ] includes a clear roadmap paragraph

### Main Sections
- [ ] explain why each algorithm family matters
- [ ] describe the core idea at a review level
- [ ] include limitations and assumptions
- [ ] avoid turning into paper summaries
- [ ] connect to other sections where useful

### Practical Constraints Section
- [ ] discusses noise, scale, and resource limits
- [ ] includes hidden costs such as input/output assumptions
- [ ] helps reinterpret earlier algorithm claims

### Classical Comparison Section
- [ ] uses serious classical baselines
- [ ] avoids weak comparisons
- [ ] distinguishes theoretical advantage from practical competitiveness

### Conclusion
- [ ] synthesizes instead of repeating
- [ ] restates the main analytical message
- [ ] ends with a cautious outlook

---

## Terminology Checks
Use terms consistently across the paper:
- quantum advantage
- theoretical speedup
- practical feasibility
- resource requirements
- classical baselines
- NISQ
- fault-tolerant quantum computing
- variational quantum algorithms

---

## Citation Checks
- [ ] mark all strong factual claims
- [ ] use [FOUNDATIONAL REF] for milestone claims
- [ ] use [RECENT REVIEW] for current field summaries
- [ ] use [RESOURCE ESTIMATION REF] for feasibility/resource claims
- [ ] use [CLASSICAL COMPARISON REF] where comparison claims are made
- [ ] do not invent references

---

## Sentence-Level Checks
Revise sentences that are:
- too long
- repetitive
- too absolute
- too vague
- overly promotional
- carrying too many claims at once

Prefer sentences that are:
- direct
- compact
- precise
- logically connected

---

## Final Checklist
- [ ] reads like a review article
- [ ] synthesizes rather than lists
- [ ] maintains academic tone
- [ ] clearly distinguishes theory from practice
- [ ] discusses limitations throughout
- [ ] uses consistent terminology
- [ ] includes citation placeholders where needed
- [ ] is close to journal-style polish
