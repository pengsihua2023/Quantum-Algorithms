# 10. Comparison with Classical Algorithms

## 10.1 Why comparison matters
The significance of a quantum algorithm cannot be judged in isolation. It must be evaluated relative to the best available classical approaches, with attention to assumptions, cost models, accuracy requirements, and implementation conditions.

## 10.2 Cases with strong theoretical separation
Some quantum algorithms provide clear formal improvements under standard models, including polynomial or stronger speedups for specific structured problems. These cases remain foundational to the intellectual significance of the field.

## 10.3 Cases where the practical picture is less clear
In other areas, especially heuristic NISQ methods and some machine-learning-related proposals, the relationship between quantum and classical performance remains unsettled. Theoretical appeal does not by itself establish empirical competitiveness.

## 10.4 Toward fair evaluation standards
A fair comparison should account for input preparation, output interpretation, hardware limitations, runtime model, and quality of classical baselines. Without such standards, claims about “quantum advantage” risk becoming ambiguous or overstated.
